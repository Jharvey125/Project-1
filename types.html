Types of meditation

mindfulness meditation


Mindfulness meditation is when we pay close attention to all thoughts that are passing through our mind good, bad maybe even intrusive however, we don’t sit and judge them or allow ourselves to let them in we simply watch them and take note and compassion into how we are feeling, in which combines our concentration with a deeper awareness of our consciousness.


spiritual meditation


Spiritual mediation is ideal for those who are wanting to grow deeper into their faith and grow a stronger connection to their higher power, this can be practised amongst multiple religions for example: Christian contemplative prayer and Jewish kabbalistic practises. During of which we sit and let ourselves seek God through contemplation and solitude.


focused meditation


Focused mediation is as obvious as it sounds, while we meditate, we focus upon something of which requires us to use one of our five senses. For example, you may close your eyes and listened to the sound of a gong or watch a candle flame and its movements while counting our breathes and calming/quieting the mind.


movement meditation


Mediation doesn’t mean just sitting down and focusing on our breathes, we can also mediate through moving our body. You may want to go for a walk-in nature and while doing so you can meditate through focusing on the things around such as the sound of the birds in order to quiet the mind and push away negativity. Furthermore, you can get yourself involved in tai chi which as a traditional Chinese practice where you use slow, gentle movements to get the body going as well as to relax you.

However, if this doesn’t suit you there’s other options such as:


  • Gardening
  • Art
  • Qi gong
  • yogo
  • Ect...

visualization meditation


Visualization meditation is used for us to enhance the feelings of relaxation and calmness by closing our eyes and visualising scenes which are positive, we also ensure that we use out 5 senses; touch, smell, sight, hearing and taste in order to make these visualisations more vivid in turn inducing a stronger effect. For example, you may find yourself happiest and calmest at the beach so you sit and picture yourself there, what does the ocean sound like? can you feel the sand beneath you? can you tase the saltiness of the water in the air? All of these are amazing to enhance calmness and improve your focus and motivation as well as lead you to inner peace.
